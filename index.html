What is Tech Jobs Academy?

Tech Jobs Academy is an innovative approach to a hard problem: the skills gap. The program is a framework through which talented people acquire in-demand IT skills, enabling them to gain employment with local businesses – including hundreds of thousands of Microsoft partner companies – in need of precisely these 21st century skills.

Who is eligible to attend?

Tech Jobs Academy was created for those who have everything but opportunity. The program aims to expand access to in-demand technical training to unemployed or underemployed adults who are passionate about launching careers in technology.

How does the program work?

The program employs a hybrid learning model that blends hard skills and soft skills, pairing a focus on real-world projects with labs, lecture, and certification exams. Government-backed workforce training programs provide funding, which flows to an educational institution delivering Microsoft-developed course modules and testing. Throughout the program, each participant completes roughly 1,000 hours of high-quality skills development.

What does a participant gain?

Participants gain deep subject matter expertise and have the opportunity to become Microsoft Certified Professionals (MCP), Microsoft Certified Solutions Associates (MCSA), and Microsoft Certified Solutions Experts (MCSE), which have been shown to increase employability and compensation. In addition to the specific IT skills gained, graduates develop strong collaboration skills and technical writing abilities.

Where does the program stand?

In its 2016 pilot iteration in New York City, Microsoft collaborated with the local workforce investment board and a local educational provider to deliver Cloud and Server Administration skills (other areas of focus could include Cybersecurity or Database Administration). Through a rigorous process, 500 applicants narrowed to 50 selected trainees, producing 47 graduates.

What specific skills do graduates have?

Cloud and Server Administration-focused pilots include:

  • Installing and deploying Windows Server 2012
  • Automation using PowerShell 3.0
  • Active Directory user and group policy management
  • Building and deploying a private cloud environment using System Center 2012
  • Building, installing, and configuring a virtualized Windows server environment via VMM and Operations Manager
  • Technical writing and documentation

How do local businesses benefit?

Tech Jobs Academy graduates fill a variety of full-time IT positions, particularly within small to medium-sized companies in the local metropolitan area, at no hiring cost to the company. The program is built to be responsive to the needs of local businesses, so that the specific skills taught can be easily adjusted as industry needs change.

Why Tech Jobs Academy?

Tech Jobs Academy upskills local residents, providing local businesses with valuable human resources at reasonable costs, driving economic growth and a more vibrant community by changing lives for the better.
